"...tinkle, tinkle, tinkle ..." It still seems like a miracle to me that I heard Lilly's faint little voice in the thick snow drifting along the Rhine. But my initial relief at having found the child has turned to horror, because it is now clear that Lilly has not only contracted pneumonia during the hours in the freezing cold. She suffers from a dangerous autoimmune disease, the hallmark of which is a dramatic decrease in white blood cells! And the usual therapies do not work for Lilly! Only a stem cell transplant can help the child, but the search for a suitable donor seems to be hopeless. "I'll sing you your favorite Christmas song for Christmas, Aunt Bergen," Lilly promised me. But for her to keep this promise, a second great miracle would have to happen - a real Christmas miracle ...
