Two days later, they were greeted in the pediatric intensive care unit with the news that Lilly had also developed bacterial meningitis, which was also due to her immune deficiency. "But don't worry, we'll get a handle on that too," Dr. Gellert reassured the horrified parents. However, she did not conceal from them that Lilly's life was in danger at the moment. The parents were desperate, as were the grandparents. They tried to encourage each other and distracted themselves with work. Marissa waited on them full time at the store those days. They took turns looking after Lilly, who had to lie in the isolation tent already mentioned by Dr. Gellert. Only through a pane of glass could they wave to the little girl and give her a kiss.
