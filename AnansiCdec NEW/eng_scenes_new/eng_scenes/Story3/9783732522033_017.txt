This is how things stood at St. Mary's Abbey on the day Count Harro was acquitted for lack of evidence. Mother Anna was beside herself. She knew what humiliation and pain this conditional acquittal must have in store for Count Harro, and was disconsolate that she could not help him. So firmly and surely had she believed in his complete acquittal. She wept heartbreakingly, and Griseldis had to comfort her.
