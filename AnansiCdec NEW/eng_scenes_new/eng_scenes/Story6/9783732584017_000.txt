Patrem sat in his tower chamber, magnifying glasses on his nose, and gazed with bated breath at the exposed piece of positronic brain on the table. With one hand he held the hair-thin tweezers, with the other the tiny pulse generator. Just a small touch, a minimal deviation, and all the preliminary work would have been for nothing. He pressed the hard edges of his lips together, focused once more on the point to be hit, and finally placed the pulse generator exactly between the two nerve endings on the contact plate. Elenore's eyelids twitched. For a moment she opened her eyes and looked at him. Then the light behind the pupil went out. "Almost," the archivist whispered. "Almost you could have stayed."
